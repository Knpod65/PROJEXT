# Futures Intelligence Guide

## Purpose

Futures Intelligence supports long-range thinking about possible institutional futures.

It helps leaders think ahead without confusing scenario planning with guaranteed outcomes.

## What Matters Most

- Scenario direction
- Long-term risk pressure
- Structural weakness
- Strategic resilience
- Whether the institution is building capacity for future change

## Metric Interpretation

- A futures signal often reflects pattern movement over time
- The exact number matters less than the direction and implication
- Scenario differences should be read as alternatives, not predictions of certainty
- A persistent weakness in one scenario can inform strategy even if it is not immediate

## Urgency Levels

- Green: strategic monitoring only
- Amber: review assumptions and preparedness
- Red: use the signal in planning and mitigation work

## Warning Interpretation

- Futures warnings are about preparedness, not immediate failure
- A weak scenario signal may mean the institution needs more resilience
- A repeated concern deserves strategic review

## Operational Meaning

This dashboard answers: what kind of future should we prepare for?

## Governance Meaning

Futures intelligence supports long-term stewardship and resilience planning.

## Recommended Actions

1. Read the scenario carefully.
2. Identify the assumption behind the signal.
3. Compare the scenario with current institutional capacity.
4. Use the output to shape planning, not to replace current operational judgment.

## Escalation Triggers

- Strategic resilience gap
- Repeated future risk signal
- Structural weakness across scenarios
- Planning assumptions no longer match reality

## Common Misunderstandings

- Futures intelligence is not a promise.
- Scenario planning is not the same as forecasting.
- Long-range output should guide preparation, not immediate panic.

## Simplified Explanation

This dashboard helps leaders think about which future paths the institution should be ready for.
