# Predictive Intelligence Guide

## Purpose

Predictive Intelligence shows what may happen next if the current pattern continues.

It is a planning tool, not a certainty engine.

## What Matters Most

- Direction of change
- Confidence level
- Forecasted pressure
- Early warning signals
- Whether intervention can still change the outcome

## Metric Interpretation

- A rising risk signal means the system may become strained later
- A confidence score shows how cautiously the result should be used
- A repeated forecast pattern is often more meaningful than a single result
- A short-term warning may matter more than a long-range estimate when planning an active period

## Urgency Levels

- Green: observe normally
- Amber: prepare earlier than usual
- Red: take preventive action now

## Warning Interpretation

- Low confidence means the forecast should be used carefully
- High pressure means intervention may be needed sooner
- A forecast warning is about possibility, not guarantee

## Operational Meaning

This dashboard answers: what should we prepare for next?

## Governance Meaning

Predictive intelligence should help leaders prepare responsibly without overstating certainty.

## Recommended Actions

1. Check the forecast direction.
2. Review the confidence and assumptions.
3. Compare the forecast with current operations.
4. Prepare a preventive response if the signal is strong.
5. Escalate if the forecast points to likely overload or failure.

## Escalation Triggers

- Repeated forecasted overload
- Low confidence paired with high risk
- Strong pressure that affects an active period
- Forecasted failure of coverage, staffing, or readiness

## Common Misunderstandings

- Prediction is not proof.
- A low-confidence signal should not be treated as certainty.
- A forecast only matters when linked to an actual decision.

## Simplified Explanation

This dashboard warns the team early so they can prepare before the problem appears in operations.
