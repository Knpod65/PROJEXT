# Distribution Staff Manual

## Purpose

Distribution staff manage the movement of papers, materials, and handoff items needed for exam operations.

The role is about accuracy, timing, and traceability.

## Responsibilities

- Prepare distribution items
- Confirm what must be delivered and to whom
- Track handoff completion
- Watch for missing materials or delays
- Escalate when delivery risk affects exam readiness

## Operational Goals

- Deliver the right material to the right place on time
- Prevent missing or duplicate handoffs
- Reduce distribution confusion
- Keep the handoff trace complete
- Avoid delays that affect active exam flow

## Daily Workflow

1. Review the distribution queue.
2. Check what materials are expected.
3. Confirm destination, timing, and owner.
4. Complete handoff and record the result.
5. Escalate if any item is missing, delayed, or mismatched.

## Key Dashboards

- Dashboard
- Workload Duty Analytics
- Operational Health
- Governance Cockpit if distribution is blocked by policy or trace issues

## Common Actions

- Confirm a delivery list
- Verify a handoff destination
- Mark completion
- Report missing or delayed materials
- Coordinate with room or exam staff

## Governance Responsibilities

Distribution staff must preserve a clear trace of what was handed over, when, and by whom.

This makes the workflow auditable and reduces dispute later.

## Common Mistakes

- Delivering based on assumption instead of the current queue
- Skipping a trace entry for a handoff
- Confusing a partial delivery with a completed delivery
- Failing to escalate a missing item quickly

## Dashboard Interpretation

- High pressure or delay signals mean the distribution chain may need help
- Missing-item warnings mean the material path is incomplete
- Repeated delay patterns often indicate a process bottleneck

## Operational Stress Points

- Peak exam delivery windows
- Last-minute material changes
- Handoff delays
- Incomplete destination data
- Unexpected material shortages

## Troubleshooting

If distribution does not match the expected plan:

- Check the queue and destination data
- Confirm the handoff owner
- Compare against the current exam or room schedule
- Escalate quickly if a delivery might affect an active exam

## Best Practices

- Treat each handoff as a traceable event
- Confirm before you move material
- Escalate uncertainty early
- Prefer a short delay over an incorrect delivery
