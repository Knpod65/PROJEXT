# Executive Manual

## Purpose

Executives use EMS to understand operational health, strategic risk, workload balance, and whether the institution is ready to move forward.

This role should see the system as a decision aid, not as a technical control panel.

## Responsibilities

- Review readiness and risk signals
- Watch for trends that affect institutional stability
- Confirm whether governance and operations are aligned
- Escalate problems that could affect teaching continuity, exam delivery, or institutional trust
- Use summaries to guide decisions, not to micromanage execution

## Operational Goals

- Keep the institution moving safely
- Detect risk early
- Avoid surprises during peak operations
- Make decisions based on trend and severity, not isolated alerts
- Preserve trust in the platform’s reporting and governance controls

## Daily Workflow

1. Open the executive dashboard.
2. Check readiness, workload pressure, major blockers, and unresolved governance items.
3. Review whether the current period is stable or drifting.
4. Open supporting dashboards only when a signal needs clarification.
5. Escalate any issue that may affect publication, staffing, fairness, or continuity.

## Key Dashboards

- Executive Analytics
- Governance Cockpit
- Operational Health
- Workload Duty Analytics
- Audit Explorer
- Optimization-related views when a decision depends on them

## Common Actions

- Confirm whether the institution is on track
- Review major risk trends
- Ask for clarification on an unexpected shift
- Approve escalation or follow-up review
- Check whether the data reflects the current operational reality

## Governance Responsibilities

Executives must ensure that governance is being used to protect trust, not to slow work without reason.

They should respect approval chains, publication gates, and audit requirements.

## Common Mistakes

- Reading a dashboard as a performance scorecard instead of a risk and readiness view
- Asking for more detail than is necessary for the decision
- Ignoring trend drift because the current value still looks acceptable
- Treating every alert as a crisis

## Dashboard Interpretation

- Green usually means the area is stable enough for normal operation
- Amber usually means watch closely and prepare action
- Red usually means the decision should not move forward without review
- Trend direction matters as much as the current value
- Repeated alerts matter more than a single isolated event

## Operational Stress Points

- Peak exam periods
- Publication deadlines
- Staffing shortages
- Sudden workload imbalance
- Governance backlog
- Conflicting signals across dashboards

## Troubleshooting

If the executive view looks inconsistent:

- Check the period and role scope first
- Open the operational dashboard that owns the underlying data
- Confirm whether the issue is a real operational problem or a reporting delay
- Escalate to the responsible operational or governance owner if the issue persists

## Best Practices

- Use the executive view for direction, not detail
- Focus on the few signals that can change the plan
- Ask for explanation when a trend changes sharply
- Prefer calm escalation over reactive intervention
