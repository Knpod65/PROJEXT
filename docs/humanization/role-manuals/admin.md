# Admin Manual

## Purpose

Admins coordinate the full operational system.

This role manual is written to help an admin understand what to open first, what signals matter, and what actions are safe to take.

## Daily Workflow

1. Open the dashboard and check readiness, blockers, and active alerts.
2. Review governance items that require approval or publication.
3. Review workload and operational health signals.
4. Confirm the schedule, room, and staffing state before peak activity.
5. Escalate any unresolved risk that may affect publication or delivery.

## Most Important Pages

- Dashboard
- Admin Intelligence
- Governance Cockpit
- Executive Analytics
- Operational Health
- Audit Explorer
- Workload Duty Analytics
- Optimization Trace Explorer

## What Admins Should Watch For

- Publication blockers
- Missing room or invigilator coverage
- Workload imbalance
- Governance items that are waiting too long
- Audit trails that do not match the expected workflow path

## Common Mistakes

- Treating a warning banner as a technical issue when it is actually a workflow issue
- Looking at a metric without checking the role or period scope
- Approving a release before the dependent operational signals are clean
- Confusing a summary dashboard with a detailed incident view

## Best Practices

- Start with the highest-risk items first.
- Read the dashboard as a decision list, not a report card.
- Use audit and trace views to confirm the cause before changing a workflow.
- Keep governance approval separate from operational confirmation.

## Escalation Flow

If a problem affects publication, access, or staff safety, escalate immediately.

If the issue is only a data mismatch, confirm the source system before changing the workflow.

## Governance Responsibility

Admins are responsible for keeping the operational system trustworthy.

That means ensuring that approvals, visibility, publication gates, and audit records remain consistent with policy.
