# ESQ Manual

## Purpose

ESQ users support governance, signature flow, and structured oversight across exam operations.

This role often sits between operational execution and formal approval.

## Responsibilities

- Review items that require sign-off or governance attention
- Confirm whether workflows are ready for progression
- Watch for missing approvals or incomplete traceability
- Coordinate with admin or secretary roles when a workflow is blocked
- Keep governance decisions aligned with policy and audit expectations

## Operational Goals

- Prevent unsafe publication
- Keep approval flow moving without bypassing controls
- Ensure traceability is complete before action is taken
- Reduce backlog in the approval chain
- Keep the institution compliant and operational at the same time

## Daily Workflow

1. Open the governance-related dashboard or workflow queue.
2. Review pending approvals and blocked items.
3. Check whether the request is complete and traceable.
4. Sign, approve, defer, or escalate according to policy.
5. Confirm that the downstream team has what it needs to continue.

## Key Dashboards

- Governance Cockpit
- Audit Explorer
- Executive Analytics when governance trends matter
- Workflow or approval views

## Common Actions

- Sign workflow items
- Review approvals
- Validate whether an item is ready to move forward
- Escalate missing information
- Confirm the reason a task is blocked

## Governance Responsibilities

ESQ users are guardians of controlled movement.

They should never approve a task they cannot trace, understand, or justify.

## Common Mistakes

- Approving too quickly because the queue is long
- Treating a missing field as minor when it blocks compliance
- Confusing operational completion with governance readiness
- Skipping trace review before signing

## Dashboard Interpretation

- A pending item means someone still needs to act
- A blocked item means the process cannot safely move forward yet
- An audit gap means the item should not be approved without review
- A repeated block often means the workflow design needs attention

## Operational Stress Points

- Peak release windows
- End-of-period approval rush
- Conflicting policy interpretations
- Incomplete submissions
- Rapid escalation spikes

## Troubleshooting

If a workflow does not move:

- Check whether the item is missing data or another approval
- Confirm the responsible role
- Review the audit or trace path
- Escalate if the blockage is policy-related or affects publication

## Best Practices

- Treat approval as a governance responsibility, not a formality
- Use trace evidence before taking action
- Keep decisions consistent with published policy
- Escalate policy ambiguity early
