# Intelligence Users Manual

## Purpose

Intelligence users work with higher-level operational and predictive views.

They need enough context to interpret risk and forecast signals without confusing prediction with certainty.

## Responsibilities

- Review intelligence dashboards and forecasts
- Translate predictive output into operational language
- Check confidence, assumptions, and limitations
- Identify whether the signal requires action or only observation
- Escalate when a forecast suggests a future operational failure

## Operational Goals

- Understand risk before it becomes visible in operations
- Use intelligence to prepare, not panic
- Keep forecast interpretation honest and bounded
- Avoid overconfidence in predictive results
- Support planning with useful and cautious summaries

## Daily Workflow

1. Open the predictive or intelligence view.
2. Review trend, risk, and confidence signals.
3. Compare forecast output to the current operational state.
4. Identify the earliest point where intervention would matter.
5. Escalate if the forecast predicts instability, shortage, or overload.

## Key Dashboards

- Predictive Intelligence
- National Intelligence
- Futures Intelligence
- Operational Health when used as a supporting view

## Common Actions

- Review forecasted pressure
- Interpret scenario signals
- Explain likely future impact
- Flag uncertainty and assumption gaps
- Prepare a preventive response

## Governance Responsibilities

Intelligence users should keep the distinction between forecast and fact very clear.

They must avoid turning a prediction into a claim without context.

## Common Mistakes

- Treating a forecast as a certainty
- Ignoring confidence or uncertainty indicators
- Using intelligence output without checking the current operational state
- Presenting speculative outputs as if they were already confirmed

## Dashboard Interpretation

- Stronger forecast pressure means the system should prepare earlier
- Low confidence means the result should be used carefully
- Direction matters more than exact precision in many planning cases
- A forecast that repeats across runs deserves more attention

## Operational Stress Points

- Sparse historical data
- Rapidly changing conditions
- High-stakes planning windows
- Pressure to overstate certainty
- Confusion between operational and strategic horizons

## Troubleshooting

If the intelligence view seems unrealistic:

- Check source completeness
- Review confidence indicators
- Compare against current operations
- Escalate if the model assumption seems outdated

## Best Practices

- Explain what the forecast suggests, not what it guarantees
- Tie the intelligence back to an operational decision
- State limitations clearly
- Use the result to prepare a response, not to replace judgment
