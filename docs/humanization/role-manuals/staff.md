# Staff Manual

## Purpose

Staff support the operational flow of EMS across scheduling, monitoring, distribution, and room-level execution.

This manual helps staff move through the system in the order that matches the workday.

## Daily Workflow

1. Check the dashboard for today’s operational priorities.
2. Review schedule, room, and staffing readiness.
3. Handle distribution, print, check-in, or room operations as assigned.
4. Watch for blockers, missing materials, or coverage gaps.
5. Escalate any issue that may delay exam execution.

## Most Important Pages

- Dashboard
- Schedule
- Checkins
- Room Attendance
- Print Queue
- Copy Count
- Export Center
- Workload Duty Analytics if assigned to operations oversight

## What Staff Should Watch For

- Missing invigilator coverage
- Room conflicts
- Distribution failures
- Delays in printing or document handoff
- Unclear ownership of a task

## Common Mistakes

- Acting on a page before checking the active period or scope
- Using the wrong workflow page for a task that belongs to a different role
- Treating a warning as informational when it actually blocks the next step

## Best Practices

- Follow the operational sequence.
- Confirm the schedule before the room.
- Confirm the room before the distribution handoff.
- Confirm coverage before the exam starts.

## Escalation Flow

Escalate immediately when a room, distribution, or attendance issue could affect an active exam.

Escalate to governance only when the issue affects approval, publication, or audit integrity.

## Governance Responsibility

Staff are responsible for accurate execution of the operational workflow.

Their job is not only to finish tasks, but to finish them in the correct order with traceable handoff.
