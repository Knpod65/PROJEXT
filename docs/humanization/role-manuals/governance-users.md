# Governance Users Manual

## Purpose

Governance users oversee approval, audit, traceability, and publication control.

Their job is to make sure EMS remains trustworthy while still allowing the institution to move.

## Responsibilities

- Review governance items and audit evidence
- Validate workflow completeness
- Confirm publication readiness
- Protect traceability and policy compliance
- Resolve or escalate exceptions

## Operational Goals

- Keep approvals understandable
- Prevent unsafe releases
- Maintain consistent governance rules
- Reduce policy confusion
- Preserve a clean audit trail

## Daily Workflow

1. Review pending governance items.
2. Check audit context and workflow state.
3. Determine whether the item is ready, blocked, or incomplete.
4. Approve, reject, defer, or escalate.
5. Confirm downstream effects on operations or publication.

## Key Dashboards

- Governance Cockpit
- Audit Explorer
- Executive Analytics for governance trends
- Workflow state and publication views

## Common Actions

- Review approval queues
- Open audit traces
- Compare current state with expected policy
- Escalate ambiguous cases
- Confirm publication gates are satisfied

## Governance Responsibilities

Governance users are the system’s control layer.

They should ensure that every release, publication, and exception can be explained later.

## Common Mistakes

- Treating audit review as optional
- Approving when the trace is incomplete
- Allowing urgency to override policy
- Confusing operational progress with governance readiness

## Dashboard Interpretation

- Pending items mean governance work remains
- Red items mean a policy or trace issue is blocking progress
- Audit gaps mean the path is not yet safe to publish
- Escalation spikes often signal process confusion or hidden dependency issues

## Operational Stress Points

- Publication deadlines
- Policy exceptions
- Backlogged approvals
- Incomplete trace data
- Cross-role disagreement on whether something is ready

## Troubleshooting

If the governance view is hard to interpret:

- Open the underlying workflow or audit record
- Check whether the item is blocked by policy or missing data
- Confirm whether the issue is local or institution-wide
- Escalate unclear policy interpretation

## Best Practices

- Keep review decisions traceable
- Prefer clear rejection or deferral over unclear approval
- Use the dashboard as a control aid, not a replacement for judgment
- Document exceptions clearly
