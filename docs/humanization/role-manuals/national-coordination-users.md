# National Coordination Users Manual

## Purpose

National coordination users monitor higher-level institutional continuity, resilience, and shared education operations.

This role needs a summary that supports strategic coordination without losing the local realities underneath it.

## Responsibilities

- Review national or multi-institution signals
- Track resilience, continuity, and risk patterns
- Monitor cross-system pressure and coordination gaps
- Assess whether a signal needs local remediation or higher-level intervention
- Escalate systemic risk to the appropriate institutional owner

## Operational Goals

- Maintain continuity across the broader system
- Identify shared failure patterns early
- Support resilience planning
- Avoid overreaching into local operational decisions
- Keep higher-level oversight grounded in evidence

## Daily Workflow

1. Open the national or federation intelligence view.
2. Review resilience, pressure, and trend signals.
3. Check whether the issue is isolated, regional, or systemic.
4. Identify which institution or unit owns the local action.
5. Escalate only when the issue crosses the coordination threshold.

## Key Dashboards

- National Intelligence
- Futures Intelligence
- Predictive Intelligence
- Federation intelligence views
- Executive Analytics when strategic comparison is needed

## Common Actions

- Review regional patterns
- Compare resilience across institutions
- Flag shared risk or bottlenecks
- Support coordinated response planning
- Escalate strategic continuity issues

## Governance Responsibilities

National coordination users must preserve accurate boundaries between local operation, institutional coordination, and strategic oversight.

They should not flatten the system into a single level of control.

## Common Mistakes

- Treating a local problem as a national one without evidence
- Ignoring variation in institutional context
- Using high-level summaries without checking the source view
- Overstating certainty in long-range planning

## Dashboard Interpretation

- Repeated cross-system pressure can indicate a resilience issue
- A sharp local spike may not need national intervention
- Forecast signals should be treated as planning support, not certainty
- Coordination warnings should be read as action requests, not blame markers

## Operational Stress Points

- Major examination periods
- Regional staffing pressure
- Long-range planning windows
- Competing institutional priorities
- Cross-unit continuity events

## Troubleshooting

If the national view feels too abstract:

- Open the lower-level federation or institutional view
- Check the current scope and timeframe
- Confirm the ownership of the issue
- Escalate only after the evidence is clear

## Best Practices

- Keep coordination clear and bounded
- Use evidence to prioritize intervention
- Separate planning from immediate operational response
- Treat continuity as a shared responsibility
