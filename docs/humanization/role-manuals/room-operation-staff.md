# Room Operation Staff Manual

## Purpose

Room operation staff manage the local execution of exams inside a room or set of rooms.

This role needs to know what is happening now, what should happen next, and when the room is no longer safe to proceed without help.

## Responsibilities

- Check room readiness
- Confirm invigilator presence and materials
- Monitor room status during active sessions
- Respond to local issues quickly
- Escalate room conflicts, missing coverage, or operational failure

## Operational Goals

- Keep the room ready before the exam begins
- Keep the room stable during the exam
- Prevent avoidable interruptions
- Reduce confusion between room-level and institution-level issues
- Escalate fast when a room cannot continue safely

## Daily Workflow

1. Review assigned rooms and the current schedule.
2. Confirm materials, staff, and room status.
3. Open the room operation view.
4. Watch for attendance, timing, or coverage problems.
5. Escalate if the room cannot proceed as planned.

## Key Dashboards

- Dashboard
- Room Attendance
- Operational Health
- Workload Duty Analytics if role scope includes oversight

## Common Actions

- Confirm room readiness
- Check attendance or presence
- Record local status
- Request help for a room issue
- Escalate a conflict or missing assignment

## Governance Responsibilities

Room operation staff must keep local operations truthful.

If the room state changes, the system state should be updated as soon as possible.

## Common Mistakes

- Starting a room operation before confirming readiness
- Assuming staff presence without checking the current status
- Treating a room conflict as a small local delay
- Leaving the room state stale after a change

## Dashboard Interpretation

- Green means the room is ready or stable
- Amber means attention is needed soon
- Red means the room may not be safe to continue without intervention

## Operational Stress Points

- Late arrivals
- Missing invigilators
- Room conflicts
- Material delays
- Sudden changes in room assignment

## Troubleshooting

If the room view looks wrong:

- Check the current schedule and room assignment
- Confirm the staff and material status
- Compare the room view with the operational plan
- Escalate if the room state does not match reality

## Best Practices

- Keep the room view updated
- Confirm before the exam starts
- Use the dashboard to spot trouble early
- Escalate before the room becomes unstable
